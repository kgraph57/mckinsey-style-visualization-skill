# Investment Memo Draft v2

## Strategic question

Should leadership approve a $2M implementation capacity investment this quarter?

## Insight headline

Approve the $2M implementation investment: it closes the capacity gap and creates room for forecast demand.

## Recommended visualization

Before/after capacity comparison with investment callout.

## Slide spec

- Canvas: 16:9 landscape.
- Layout: two vertical bars in the center, investment callout on the left, implication box on the right.
- Left bar:
  - Label: `Current capacity`.
  - Primary number: `12 deployments / quarter`.
  - Annotation: `67% of forecast demand`.
- Right bar:
  - Label: `Post-investment capacity`.
  - Primary number: `20 deployments / quarter`.
  - Annotation: `111% of forecast demand`.
- Investment callout:
  - `$2M implementation team investment`.
- Direct label over gap:
  - `Capacity moves from 6-deployment shortfall to 2-deployment buffer`.
- Implication box:
  - `Decision: approve investment before raising enterprise targets`.
- Source note:
  - `Source: user-provided capacity and investment assumptions; payback period not provided`.

## Data and assumptions

- Investment size is $2M.
- Current capacity is 12 deployments per quarter.
- Post-investment capacity is 20 deployments per quarter.
- Forecast demand is 18 deployments per quarter.
- Current coverage is 67%, calculated as 12 divided by 18.
- Post-investment coverage is 111%, calculated as 20 divided by 18.
- Payback period, gross margin, and implementation hiring timeline are missing.

## Quality check

- Strategy: recommendation directly answers the approval question.
- Data integrity: values, calculations, missing payback assumptions, and source note are explicit.
- Visual hierarchy: before/after bars, investment callout, and implication box create the reading order.
- Portability: canvas, layout, labels, annotations, and source note are renderable.
- Marketplace safety: original, source-aware, and based on user-provided assumptions.
