# Market Entry Draft v2

## Strategic question

Should the board approve Japan market entry now, or sequence capacity investment before scale-up?

## Insight headline

Japan entry should be sequenced: build implementation capacity before scaling enterprise sales.

## Recommended visualization

Market entry contrast diagram plus implementation decision gate.

## Slide spec

- Canvas: 16:9 landscape.
- Layout: split comparison with a vertical divider.
- Left column: `Existing market`
  - Primary number: `70% enterprise adoption`.
  - Supporting bars: `high adoption`, `known sales motion`, `existing delivery capacity`.
- Right column: `Japan`
  - Primary number: `15% early adoption`.
  - Supporting bars: `low adoption`, `high localization burden`, `capacity investment required`.
- Add an implication box in the lower-right corner:
  - `Decision: sequence entry`.
  - `Approve capacity investment before raising Japan sales targets`.
- Source note:
  - `Source: user-provided market assumptions; investment amount not yet provided`.

## Data and assumptions

- Existing market adoption is 70%.
- Japan early adoption is 15%.
- Implementation capacity need is directionally known, but investment amount and ROI are missing.
- The recommendation is conditional on validating capacity cost and sales-cycle assumptions.

## Quality check

- Strategy: recommendation answers the sequencing question.
- Data: values and missing investment amount are visible.
- Visual hierarchy: two primary numbers, contrast bars, and decision box.
- Portability: renderer can build the diagram from the spec.
- Safety: no copied source material or affiliation claims.
