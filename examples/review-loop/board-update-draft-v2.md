# Board Update Draft v2

## Strategic question

Should the board approve implementation capacity investment before raising enterprise sales targets?

## Insight headline

Approve implementation capacity investment before raising enterprise targets.

## Recommended visualization

Executive summary strip with embedded capacity gap visual.

## Slide spec

- Canvas: 16:9 landscape.
- Layout: three insight blocks across the top and one capacity gap bar across the bottom.
- Block 1 primary number: `$15M Q4 ARR`.
  - Direct label: `ARR grew from $10M to $15M`.
  - Annotation: `Growth engine is working`.
- Block 2 primary number: `64% AI workflow adoption`.
  - Direct label: `18% to 64% adoption`.
  - Annotation: `Operating model is scaling`.
- Block 3 primary number: `12 / 18 deployment capacity`.
  - Direct label: `Current capacity covers 67% of forecast demand`.
  - Annotation: `Capacity is the constraint`.
- Bottom implication box:
  - `Decision: approve implementation capacity investment before raising targets`.
- Source note:
  - `Source: user-provided board metrics; hiring cost and ROI assumptions not provided`.

## Data and assumptions

- ARR grew from $10M to $15M.
- AI workflow adoption grew from 18% to 64%.
- Current capacity is 12 deployments per quarter.
- Forecast demand is 18 deployments per quarter.
- Coverage is 67%, calculated as 12 divided by 18.
- Hiring cost, time-to-productivity, and ROI are missing.

## Quality check

- Strategy: the recommendation answers the board decision.
- Data integrity: values, calculation, missing assumptions, and source note are explicit.
- Visual hierarchy: three primary numbers plus a bottom implication box.
- Portability: canvas, layout, direct labels, annotations, and source note are renderable.
- Marketplace safety: original, source-aware, and based on user-provided metrics.
